# Raze Network: Privacy-Preserving DeFi on Solana

## What is Raze?

Raze Network is a privacy protocol built on Solana that enables confidential transactions and private DeFi interactions. Using zero-knowledge proofs (ZK-SNARKs), Raze allows users to shield their on-chain activity from public view while maintaining the speed and low cost benefits of the Solana ecosystem.

## Key Features

### Private Transactions
Raze uses cryptographic shielding to break the link between sender and receiver addresses, making it impossible to trace fund flows on-chain. This is critical for:
- Business confidentiality (hiding treasury movements)
- Personal privacy (protecting spending patterns)
- Institutional DeFi (confidential large-scale operations)

### Zero-Knowledge Architecture
- ZK-SNARKs for proof generation without revealing transaction data
- Solana's high TPS ensures ZK proofs don't create bottlenecks
- Compatible with existing Solana DeFi protocols

### Native Token: RAZE
- Used for governance and fee payment
- Staking for protocol rewards
- Liquidity mining incentives

## Why Privacy Matters in DeFi

Current blockchain transparency creates risks:
1. **Front-running:** MEV bots can see pending transactions and exploit them
2. **Wallet profiling:** Anyone can track your complete financial history
3. **Competitive intelligence:** Business rivals can monitor treasury movements

Raze solves these issues by bringing Ethereum-style privacy (like Tornado Cash) to Solana's high-performance environment.

## Getting Started with Raze

1. Acquire RAZE tokens via DEX (Raydium, Orca)
2. Deposit assets into the Raze shielding pool
3. Transact privately within the pool
4. Withdraw to any address without linkage to deposits

## Conclusion

Raze Network fills a critical gap in the Solana ecosystem: institutional-grade financial privacy. As DeFi matures and larger capital enters the space, privacy infrastructure like Raze becomes essential for both compliance and competitive advantage.
